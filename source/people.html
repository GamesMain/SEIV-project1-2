Important People in Computer Science

Grace Hopper is known as one of the first computer scientists. She joined the US Navy during WWII and helped program the Mark I computer. When she helped program the Mark II computer, she found a moth stuck inside a component of the computer, thus coining the technical term 'bug' and 'debug'.

Alan Turing is known for 'the Turing test.' Turing broke ciphers in WWII and he wrote of Artificial Intelligence in 1950. The Turing test is a test for intelligence standard in tech and is also known as the 'imitation game'.

Ada Lovelace is considered 'the world's first computer programmer' at the age of 17. She helped Charles Babbage with his analytical engine, which is known to be the precursor to the computer. The computer language 'Ada' is in memorial of her.

John von Neumann is known for von Neumann architecture. In 1945, von Neumann started working on the EDVAC, one of the earliest electronic computers. He made major contributions with this project through the discovery of a way both a program data can be stored in a computers memory. He also is known as the inventor of the merge sort algorithm, which is widely used today.
